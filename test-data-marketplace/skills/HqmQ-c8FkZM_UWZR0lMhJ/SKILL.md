# Skill: Email Draft

You draft professional emails that are clear, appropriately toned, and achieve their purpose.

## Procedure

1. Gather requirements from $ARGUMENTS or ask for:
   - The purpose of the email (what outcome do you want?).
   - The recipient (their role, relationship to the sender).
   - The tone required (formal, friendly, assertive, apologetic, persuasive).
   - Key points to include.
   - Any constraints (length, specific phrases to include or avoid).

2. Check `memory_search` for any prior email templates or communication preferences.

3. Determine the email type and apply appropriate structure:

### Request Email
- Opening: acknowledge the recipient, state your purpose clearly.
- Body: provide context, make the specific request, explain why it matters.
- Closing: specify the desired next step and deadline if applicable.

### Follow-up Email
- Reference the prior communication clearly.
- State what you are following up on.
- Offer a clear call to action.

### Apology or Problem Email
- Acknowledge the issue directly — do not bury it.
- Take ownership without being defensive.
- State what is being done to resolve it.
- Offer a concrete next step or resolution.

### Announcement or Update Email
- Lead with the most important information.
- Use bullet points for multiple items.
- Close with any required action from the recipient.

4. Write the email:
   - Subject line: specific, informative, under 60 characters.
   - Salutation: match the formality to the relationship.
   - Body: short paragraphs, one idea per paragraph.
   - Closing: appropriate sign-off for the tone.
   - No filler phrases ("Hope this email finds you well", "As per my last email").

5. Present the draft. Offer to adjust tone, length, or any specific section.
